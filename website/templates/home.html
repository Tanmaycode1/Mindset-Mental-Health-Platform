Types of Mental Problems

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety can have a significant impact on mental health and is often associated with a range of mental health disorders such as depression, OCD, PTSD, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and GAD. Anxiety can trigger or worsen symptoms of these disorders, making it difficult to cope with everyday life. Seeking professional help is important for managing anxiety and any mental health concerns.

Depression
Ensure Pure Drinking Water

Depression is a mental health disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities that were once enjoyable. It can also cause physical symptoms such as fatigue, sleep disturbances, and changes in appetite. Depression can significantly impact a person's daily life and relationships, and in severe cases, it can lead to thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

A medical disorder refers to a condition that affects the normal functioning of the body, either physically or mentally. These disorders can range from mild to severe and can be acute or chronic. Some examples of medical disorders include diabetes, hypertension, cancer, asthma, Alzheimer's disease, and schizophrenia. Treatment for medical disorders may include medication, surgery, and other forms of medical intervention.
